Medicine - Informatics - Biology

How NGS brings us together


With the advent of high-throughput technologies, biological sciences have witnessed a change in the methods used to generate, analyze and draw solid conclusions from big data sets. Next Generation Sequencing is the most prominent high-throughput technology. Over the years, NGS has become a central platform fostering communication and collaborations between experts from very different fields.

The symposium "Medicine - Informatics - Biology. How NGS brings us together" will present excellent research made possible through the virtuous cross-contamination between medicine, biology and information technology. In addition, the symposium will address challenges in interdisciplinary communication and education and give opportunity for further mutual learning.


Present your interdisciplinary research

In frame of the symposium researchers who successfully combine biology and informatics have the possibility to present their research in a talk or one of the poster sessions. We especially invite young scientists to make use of this opportunity.


Ask your questions

Is there something that you have always wanted to ask an expert in biology or informatics/statistics? We have reserved two afternoon sessions for a format to learn from each other, called “ask the expert”. Experts in a specific field – independent of career status – often need to learn in a different field. The symposium will assemble experts with an informatics background as well as those with biology-related expertise, giving the unique opportunity to address your questions to an expert of the other field. Therefore during the registration process, indicate your specific expertise as well as the questions you would like to ask another expert during the symposium. We will match these and give time and room to group learning.


Let’s think about interdisciplinary training and communication

How can we advance interdisciplinary biology/bioinformatics training? How are you educating young scientists in both fields? What were you missing during your education? Which obstacles need to be worked on to enhance communication between wet-lab biologists and informaticians? In a World Café format the symposium will search for answers to these questions and generate perspectives for future educative formats.


Let’s meet

Of course, the symposium will give plenty of opportunity for communication and networking during coffee breaks and social evenings. The symposium is open to PhD students, post-doctoral researchers and group leaders.

You can use this web platform to register to the symposium, submit and manage your application for a talk or a poster. We look forward to hear about your awesome research!

The symposium "Medicine - Informatics - Biology. How NGS brings us together" is generously funded by the Joachim Herz Stiftung.
