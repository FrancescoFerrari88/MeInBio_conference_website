3. Note 

We take the private sphere of our visitors very seriously and collect only data that are necessary for organizing the Symposium "Medicine - Informatics - Biology. How NGS brings us together" and optimizing the functionality and usability of this web page. Insofar as the processing of personal data is necessary within the context of tasks lying within the responsibility of the University of Freiburg or a task  that is in the public interest, the processing is conducted on the basis of § 6 para. 1 lit. e of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (EU-GDPR) in conjunction with § 4 of the State Data Protection Act.

4. Collection of personal data 

4.1.1. Description and category of data

When you visit this web page you send data to the web server via your web browser. The following data are retained temporarily in a log file during an ongoing connection only in the case of an error:

  •  ♣ IP address of the requesting computer
  •  ♣ Date and time of the access
  •  ♣ Name, URL, and amount of transferred data from the requested file
  •  ♣ Access status (requested file transferred, not found, etc.)
  •  ♣ Browser type and operating system (if sent by the requesting web browser)
  •  ♣ Web page from which access was obtained (if sent by the requesting web browser)


The data in this log file are processed as follows:

  • ♣ In individual cases, i.e., reported defects, errors, and security incidents, a manual analysis is conducted.


During the registration process you need to provide your name and e-mail address to enable communication with the Symposium organizers. For active participation in the Symposium you need to provide details on your institution, your research and your participation in certain program events. These data will be stored on a passward protected server area of the University of Freiburg. Only the symposium organizing team will be able to access the server area.  


4.1.2. Purpose 

The system needs to temporarily store the user’s IP address to enable the website to be sent to the user’s computer. The user’s IP address must be retained for the duration of the session.  The data are stored in a log file as a means of ensuring the proper functioning of the website. In addition, we use the data to optimize the website and guarantee the security of our information technology systems. The IP addresses included in the log entries are not combined with other retained data unless there are actual indications that there has been a disruption of proper operation. These purposes also constitute our legitimate interest in processing data in accordance with § 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation). Your name, Email address, Institution name and participation in events will be used for organization of the Symposium "Medicine - Informatics - Biology. How NGS brings us together". Your name, Email address and Institution name can be published on the web site and in the printed Symposium program.


4.1.3. Legal basis

 The legal basis for the temporary retention of the data and the log files is § 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR.


4.1.4. Recipient

 In the case that investigative measures are initiated due to attacks on our information technology system, the data and log files named above under 4.1. may be passed on to state investigative bodies (e.g., police, public prosecutor).  The same applies if these bodies or courts direct inquiries at the university and the university is obligated to comply with them. Your name, institution and contribution to the symposium can be included in reports to the funding organizations, Joachim Herz foundation and German Research foundation (Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft, DFG). Your name, Email address and Institution name can be published on the web site and in the printed Symposium program.


4.1.5. Duration of data retention 

The data are deleted as soon as they are no longer needed to achieve the purpose for which they were collected. In the case of data collected to make the website available, this is the case at the end of the relevant session.  The data retained in log files are deleted after seven days. Your name, Email address, Institution name and participation in events will be stored until all reports to the funding bodies have been delivered and accepted.


   4.1.6. Consequences of nondisclosure, possibility of objection or removal

   The collection of data for the purpose of making the website available and the storage of the data in log files is absolutely necessary for the operation of the website. Users who do not want their data to be processed as described will not be able to actively participate in the symposium "Medicine - Informatics - Biology. How NGS brings us together".
